Problem  ·  infantile spasms from pose-only video

2 / 7
Task — binary detection of infantile spasms (West syndrome) from 5-second segments of anonymised MediaPipe Pose: $150 \times 33 \times 5$ tensors of x, y, z, visibility, presence.

Why infantile spasms. Defining seizure type of West syndrome; onset 3–24 months; brief (1–3 s) flexor/extensor/mixed contraction, often clustered. Time-to-treatment strongly predicts cognitive outcome.

Why home video. First observed by caregivers, not clinicians. Home-video review is part of the standard diagnostic pathway.

Why pose only.

  • Privacy: pose strips identity, room, clothing.
  • Compute: a few hundred floats / frame, not millions.
  • Robustness: invariant to lighting, clothing, background.

Ceiling: facial cues, vital signs and audio are not in the pose stream.

Approach  ·  end-to-end pipeline

3 / 7

Two complementary backbones.

  • CNN-GRU: recurrent inductive bias, models temporal order via hidden state.
  • Multi-scale TCN: parallel dilated convolutions covering the full 150-frame window.

5-fold StratifiedGroupKFold grouped by patient ID — leakage-free.

7-variant TTA.

Method  ·  features, models, training

4 / 7

Preprocessing. Forward/back-fill NaN frames; hip-center; torso-length normalisation. Preserve binary NaN mask as a feature.

518-d per-frame features.

ComponentDim.
Raw landmarks (x,y,z,vis,pres)165
Velocity / Acceleration / Speed231
Joint angles (10) · Symmetry (6) · NaN17
Low-freq FFT power (broadcast)105
Total518

FFT bins ≈ 0.2–3 Hz — clonic / myoclonic rhythm at 30 fps over a 5-s window.

Training.

  • Focal loss α=0.75, γ=2.0 + WeightedRandomSampler.
  • AdamW + OneCycleLR, max_lr 5×10-3, pct_start 0.3.
  • Up to 120 epochs, early stop on val F1 (patience 25).
  • Augmentation: reverse, jitter, time-resample, frame dropout, same-class mixup.

Inference. 10 models × 7 TTA variants = 70 forward passes / sample, mean probability, threshold τ = 0.65.

Same trained ensemble runs on either pre-extracted .npy or raw .mp4 via the MediaPipe adapter — no retraining.

Discussion  ·  CV F1 0.63 → test F1 0.30

6 / 7
Five plausible contributors, ordered roughly by expected effect size:
  • Distribution shift between training-pool and test-pool patients. Grouped CV controls leakage but not pool-level shift.
  • Threshold over-fit to CV. Per-fold τ̂ spans [0.25, 0.70]; the single τ = 0.65 we ship is over-fit to a small CV sample.
  • F1 brittleness under strong class imbalance — small precision / recall shifts move F1 a lot.
  • Train / TTA augmentation mismatch. Aggressive training augmentation, near-identity TTA — the model is trained to tolerate perturbations the TTA does not produce.
  • Architectural mismatch. Full-window attention pools 5 s; spasms concentrate in 1–3 s sub-windows. MIL-style sub-window pooling (used by top entries) is a better structural prior.

Concrete next actions: (i) hold out a fold for threshold calibration; (ii) refactor inference around MIL sub-window pooling.

Future work  ·  thanks

7 / 7

Future work.

  • MeTRAbs pose front-end — metric-scale 3-D, 87 joints (hand + head detail MediaPipe lacks).
  • Multi-view triangulation via markerless dynamic extrinsic calibration — fuse synchronised views.
  • MIL sub-window pooling matched to spasm duration.
  • MaskAnyone integration — on-device home-video processing; only landmarks + label leave the premises.
  • Calibrated probabilities for cost-sensitive clinical triage.
