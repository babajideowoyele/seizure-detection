Video-Based Pediatric Seizure Detection
from Pose Landmark Sequences

An ensemble of CNN – GRU and Temporal Convolutional Networks with engineered kinematic features  ·  binary detection of infantile spasms (West syndrome) from anonymised MediaPipe Pose
Babajide Owoyele, Sharjeel Shaikh, Wim Pouw, Moritz Schneider, Stefan Konigorski, Gerard de Melo
Hasso Plattner Institute, Universität Potsdam, Germany
2026 Video-Based Seizure Detection Challenge
Section on Computational Neurology, Charité — Universitätsmedizin Berlin
Int. Conf. on AI in Epilepsy and Other Neurological Disorders, 2026

Task — binary detection of infantile spasms from 5-second pose-only segments (150 × 33 × 5 MediaPipe tensors).  Approach — 5-fold ensemble of CNN-GRU + multi-scale TCN over 518-d engineered features, focal loss, child-grouped cross-validation, 7-variant TTA.  Result — mean CV F1 0.628 / 0.635; held-out test F1 0.2975 (rank 19 of 26; top score 0.4502).  Code — github.com/babowoyele/seizure-detection

1.  Problem

Infantile spasms are the defining seizure type of West syndrome, an age-dependent epileptic encephalopathy with peak onset around six months of life. A spasm is a brief (1–3 s) sudden contraction of trunk and limbs — flexor, extensor, or mixed — typically clustered near sleep–wake transitions and frequently first observed by caregivers in home-video.

Two constraints shape a deployable system:

  • Privacy. Pixel-level video carries identity, room context, and clinical metadata.
  • Long recordings. Home and clinical recordings span hours to days; raw-pixel pipelines do not scale.

A pose-only intermediate — a few hundred floats per frame, invariant to lighting, clothing, and background — solves both. The 2026 challenge distributes exactly this representation.

2.  Data

Per-segment files: (150 frames, 33 landmarks, 5 channels) — anonymised MediaPipe Pose. Channels per landmark: x, y, z, visibility, presence. Filenames carry a child-ID we exploit for leakage-free grouped cross-validation.

  • ≈5 s at 30 fps per segment.
  • Strong class imbalance toward non-seizure.
  • Frame-level missingness handled with forward/back-fill and an explicit binary mask feature.

3.  Preprocessing

  • NaN handling. Forward- then back-fill missing frames; preserve the binary NaN mask as a feature.
  • Hip-centering. Translate so the mid-hip point (landmarks 23, 24) sits at the origin per frame — translation-invariant.
  • Torso normalisation. Divide x,y,z by the per-frame mid-shoulder→mid-hip distance — scale-invariant against camera-distance.

5.  Pipeline

6.  Models

CNN-GRU. Two 1-D conv layers (k=5, k=3) project 518-d → 128 channels, then a 2-layer bidirectional GRU (hidden=128), then learned attention pooling, then a 256→64→1 MLP head.

Multi-scale TCN. Five residual blocks with kernel 3 and dilations {1, 2, 4, 8, 16} — full receptive-field coverage of the 150-frame window — then attention pooling and the same MLP head.

Why two. Recurrent vs. parallel multi-scale inductive biases produce de-correlated errors and different optimal thresholds (see Results).

7.  Training

  • 5-fold StratifiedGroupKFold by child-ID — no within-subject leakage.
  • Focal loss (α=0.75, γ=2.0) over a class-balanced WeightedRandomSampler.
  • AdamW + OneCycleLR (max_lr = 5×10−3, pct_start = 0.3).
  • Up to 120 epochs, early stopping on val F1 (patience 25).
  • Augmentation: time-reverse, jitter, time-resample, frame dropout, same-class mixup.

10.  Discussion — the CV→test gap

Cross-validation F1 ≈ 0.63 → held-out 0.2975 is a substantial gap. Five plausible contributors:

  • Distribution shift between training-pool and test-pool patients; grouped CV controls leakage but not pool shift.
  • Threshold over-fit to CV folds — per-fold τ̂ spans [0.25, 0.70].
  • F1 brittleness under strong imbalance.
  • Train/test TTA mismatch: aggressive train augmentation, near-identity TTA.
  • Architectural mismatch: full-window attention may under-exploit the sub-second concentration of spasm motor energy that explicit MIL pooling captures.

11.  Future work

  • MeTRAbs as pose front-end: metric-scale, 87-joint 3-D coordinates exposing hand and head dynamics.
  • Multi-view triangulation via markerless dynamic extrinsic calibration [1] — fuse synchronised views, remove occlusion failure modes.
  • MIL-style sub-window pooling matched to 1–3 s spasm duration.
  • Held-out fold for threshold calibration (rather than CV-direct).
  • Integration with MaskAnyone [2] — caregiver home-video processed entirely on-device; only landmarks and labels leave the premises.
